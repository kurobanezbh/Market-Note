# 2026-06-17 Market Notes

## Federal Reserve Notes

As the Federal Reserve begins its first policy meeting under new Chair Kevin Warsh, the market is less focused on what the Fed will do this week and more focused on what it might signal about the months ahead. A policy surprise appears unlikely, with investors broadly expecting rates to remain unchanged. Instead, attention is centered on Warsh's inaugural press conference, which could provide the first meaningful clues about how he intends to reshape the central bank's policy framework and communication strategy.

Supporters of the current market outlook argue that the Fed has room to remain patient. Economic growth remains resilient, financial conditions are relatively stable, and policymakers can afford to gather more evidence before making any significant adjustments. If Warsh emphasizes flexibility and avoids major policy shifts, investors may interpret that as a sign of continuity rather than disruption.

However, the risks are increasingly difficult to ignore. Inflation remains well above the Fed's target, energy-related price pressures have resurfaced, and some analysts expect the updated economic projections to lean more hawkish than markets currently anticipate. Investors will be closely watching the dot plot and Warsh's comments for signs that rate cuts could be delayed or that future rate hikes remain on the table.

Ultimately, this meeting is less about today's rate decision and more about understanding the priorities of a new Fed chair. The market may get its first glimpse of whether Warsh intends to preserve the status quo or steer monetary policy in a meaningfully different direction.
